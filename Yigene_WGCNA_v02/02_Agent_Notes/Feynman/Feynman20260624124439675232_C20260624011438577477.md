# L9 Feynman - Result Falsification

## What could kill these results?

### 1. Species vs heart-rate confound (HIGH risk)
Turquoise has 1720 genes (34%). The high_heart_rate correlation (r=-0.955) is nearly identical to species correlation (r=-0.962). In this 3-species design, species and heart rate are almost perfectly confounded: Sk and Sm are both high-rate, Rn is low-rate. The convergence claim rests on module preservation (Sk-Sm Z=16.9), which shows shared co-expression structure between bat and shrew specifically. But without a 4th high-rate species or a low-rate bat/shrew, the heart-rate vs species distinction is unresolvable with this data alone.

### 2. Batch effects in subsets (MEDIUM risk)
All-sample network used batch as covariate. Atrium (n=23) and ventricle (n=48) subsets used ~0+group without batch, to avoid rank deficiency. Subset modules may retain batch signal. All-sample network is more trustworthy.

### 3. Green module unfairly excluded (MEDIUM risk)
Green (179 genes) meets criteria (a) heart-rate correlation (r=-0.52, p=3e-6) and (b) strong preservation (Z=20.2), but fails (c) no DEG overlap. Yet its GO enrichment is the most biologically specific: cardiac muscle tissue development, striated muscle development, cardiac muscle cell differentiation. The convergence criterion (c) may be too strict. Module-level co-expression convergence does not require gene-level differential expression convergence. Flag for Darwin.

### 4. Power=4 forced; auto-pick was 1 (LOW-MEDIUM risk)
Power=1 gives near-linear adjacency, typically 1-2 huge modules. Power=4 gives 5 well-sized modules. Module sizes (1720, 1440, 1257, 291, 179) are reasonable. Sensitivity comparison (power=1 vs 4) was analysis_needed #6 and has NOT been run. Gap.

### 5. nPermutations=50 (LOW risk)
50 permutations is feasibility setting. Strong modules (green Z=20, turquoise Z=17) unlikely to change qualitatively. Moderate modules (brown Z=4.5, yellow Z=2.5) could shift. Recommend 200+ for publication.
