## Core question: Do bat (Sk) and shrew (Sm) share convergent co-expression modules?

### Answer: YES, with two convergent modules and one cardiac identity module

## Module-by-module biological interpretation

### Turquoise (1720 genes) - CONVERGENT, ventricle-associated
- Strongest anti-correlation with high_heart_rate (r=-0.955)
- Strongly preserved Sk<->Sm (Z=16.9)
- Overlaps ventricle_shared_down DEG set (804 genes, OR=5.9, p_adj=1e-146)
- Biological meaning: This is the module that is DOWN-regulated in high-rate species relative to rat, specifically in ventricle. Given its size (34% of network), it likely captures the dominant transcriptional shift. The fact that it is preserved between bat and shrew means both species independently suppressed the same gene network in their ventricles. This is consistent with a "repression of slow-rate program" model: genes needed for low-rate cardiac function are coordinately downregulated in high-rate hearts.

### Brown (1257 genes) - CONVERGENT, atrium-associated
- Positive correlation with high_heart_rate (r=+0.799)
- Moderate preservation Sk<->Sm (Z=4.5)
- Overlaps atrium_shared_up DEG set (292 genes, OR=1.8, p_adj=6e-12)
- Biological meaning: This module is UP-regulated in high-rate species, specifically in atrium. The moderate (not strong) preservation suggests partial convergence: bat and shrew both upregulate this network in atrium, but the internal structure has diverged somewhat. This could represent an active remodeling program (e.g., ion channel restructuring, metabolic upregulation) that both species need in atrium but achieve through partially different gene sets.

### Green (179 genes) - CARDIAC IDENTITY MODULE, preserved but not DEG-convergent
- Anti-correlated with high_heart_rate (r=-0.52, p=3e-6)
- STRONGEST preservation of all modules (Z=20.2)
- GO enrichment: cardiac muscle tissue development, striated muscle development, cardiac muscle cell differentiation, muscle organ morphogenesis
- Key genes: Myl2, Myl3, Myh11, Irx3, Nrg1, Tcap, Bmp10, Shox2
- Biological meaning: This is a cardiac identity/development module. Its strong preservation between bat and shrew means the core cardiac transcriptional program is conserved despite their 96+ million years of divergence. The heart-rate correlation suggests this module is expressed at lower levels in high-rate hearts - consistent with a developmental/structural program being downregulated in favor of contractile/metabolic programs in high-rate species. This is NOT a convergent adaptation module; it is a conserved cardiac baseline that both high-rate species modulate similarly.

## Convergent evolution interpretation

The key finding is that Sk (Chiroptera) and Sm (Eulipotyphla), which diverged ~96 Mya and independently evolved high heart rates, share:
1. A large ventricular module (turquoise) that is coordinately suppressed in both species
2. A smaller atrial module (brown) that is coordinately activated in both species

This dual pattern (ventricle-down + atrium-up) suggests that high-rate cardiac adaptation involves chamber-specific remodeling: the ventricle shifts away from a baseline program, while the atrium activates a new program. The fact that both species arrived at this pattern independently is evidence for convergent evolution at the co-expression network level.

## What this does NOT prove

- It does not prove the same genes drive the convergence (module preservation means structure is shared, not that the same hub genes are active)
- It does not distinguish heart-rate adaptation from other shared traits (flight in bats, high metabolism in shrews)
- It does not establish causality - the modules are correlative
- The species-vs-heart-rate confound (flagged by Feynman) means we cannot rule out that these are species-specific modules that happen to correlate with heart rate
